1. Ostriches are the largest birds in the world: Ostriches are the largest
living species of bird, with males standing up to 9 feet tall and weighing
around 300 pounds. Females are slightly smaller, but still significantly larger
than most bird species.  Reference:
https://www.worldwildlife.org/species/ostrich

2. Ostriches have the largest eyes of any land animal: The ostrich has the
largest eyes of any land animal, measuring approximately 2 inches in diameter.
Their eyes are even larger than their brains!  Reference:
https://animals.mom.com/largest-eyes-land-animal-9658.html

3. Ostriches can run at speeds of up to 60 miles per hour: Ostriches are
incredibly fast runners and can reach speeds of up to 60 miles per hour. This
makes them the fastest running birds on the planet.  Reference:
https://www.audubon.org/news/ostrich-runs-faster-horse-speed-myth-busted

4. Ostriches have the ability to defend themselves: Ostriches have powerful legs
that they can use to kick potential threats. A single kick from an ostrich can
be lethal and has been known to kill predators such as lions.  Reference:
https://www.nationalgeographic.com/animals/birds/facts/ostrich

5. Ostriches have unique behaviors for incubation: Ostriches have a unique way
of incubating their eggs. Instead of a traditional nest, they dig shallow holes
in the ground and take turns sitting on the eggs. The males primarily handle
incubation during the day, while the females take over at night.  Reference:
https://a-z-animals.com/animals/ostrich/
